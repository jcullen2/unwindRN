How the record gets written

In the minutes after clock-out.

A debrief is what pilots do after a flight. Nurses hold harder nights with less ceremony — unwindRN gives the shift somewhere to land.

01

Clock out by taps

Hours, load, what tonight had — code, a loss, a good save. Counted facts, kept. “Save without another word” is always one tap, and never judged.

02

Debrief — as long as the night needs

As short as your good nights, as long as your bad ones. When it was a code and a loss, the voice companion is there — fluent in your specialty, one question at a time, like a colleague at the end of the hall.

03

On the record

Either way, tonight becomes a clean entry — the facts, the tags, and the one line worth keeping. The logbook does the rest.

Questions nurses ask

The honest answers.

Is unwindRN therapy?

No. It's a logbook with a debrief — the professional ritual, not treatment. It doesn't diagnose, doesn't label you, and doesn't pretend to be care. If a night is heavier than an app should hold, 988 is one tap away — always, never gated.

What happens to what I say in a debrief?

Your voice is transcribed on your phone — audio never leaves it. The words you speak are used to write the entry and the companion's replies, then they're gone; conversations aren't stored. The only thing kept is the entry you approve. Details in the privacy policy.

Could this cause trouble with HIPAA or my license?

It's engineered for the opposite. unwindRN never asks for patient information, deflects it if offered, and an automated scrubber strips identifiers — rooms, beds, record numbers — before anything can be saved. Your record is about your night, never a patient's identity.

Can my hospital see any of this?

No. There is no employer product, no manager dashboard, no aggregate report — those don't exist to be bought. Your record lives in your account, and account deletion removes every trace immediately.
