# In-App Copy

## Insight lines (the moat, made visible)

Pattern: **observation → meaning**, always about HER career, never a diagnosis.

- "You've held 38% more nights than this time last year."
- "Your charge shifts doubled since March — that's a promotion pattern."
- "47 shifts to #1,500."
- "February was your heaviest stretch: three codes in nine days. You held all three."
- "Four years of Tuesdays: 212 and counting."

Rules: observations and milestones, never diagnostics ("we've detected burnout risk" is banned). Self-facing patterns (fatigue, mood after night runs) may surface in-app as gentle discoveries, opt-in in tone, and are NEVER pushed as notifications or marketed.

## Milestones

Celebratory, earned, shareable. "Shift #1,500. Eleven years on the floor, on the record." Offer the share card in the same breath. No confetti-speak, no "amazing job!!" — the number is the celebration.

## Shift cards (shareable proof layer)

Format: counted facts + one optional kept line. Examples:

> **Night 3 of 3** · 12.5 hrs · Full census · Held.

> **Shift #1,500** · ICU · 11 years on the floor

Design energy: Strava activity card, not inspirational quote card. Numbers big, mood absent, patient info structurally impossible.

## Notifications

- Never guilt, never streaks, never "you missed." Skipping is invisible.
- Frame as keeping the record, timed to clock-out: "Night 3 of 3 in the books? Two minutes and it's kept."
- Milestone pushes allowed and encouraged: "Tonight was #1,000."
- Self-facing insight pushes: banned.

## Empty states & onboarding

- Day one is the backfill moment: "Tell us where your career started. A nurse eight years in has held ~1,153 shifts — your record starts there, tonight."
- Empty logbook never scolds; it waits: "The record just waits. It'll be here after your next shift."
- Set the ramp expectation honestly during onboarding: record now, patterns in a few months, a career's worth of self-knowledge over time.

## Debrief companion register

- A colleague at the end of the hall, fluent in the specialty. One question at a time. No clinical labels, no chirp, no therapized phrasing ("hold space," "sit with that" — banned).
- Elasticity is the design: taps-only exit ("Save without another word") is always one tap and never judged; the voice companion goes as deep as the nurse wants.
- Deflects patient identifiers by design; never asks for them.
- If a night is heavier than an app should hold: surface 988 immediately, plainly, never gated. This is the one moment where the register softens — brief, direct, human. Then stop selling anything.

## Error states & settings

Same voice at small scale. Settings copy states the inversion as fact: "Export everything. Delete everything. Both are always one tap." Errors are plain and unapologetic-but-honest: "That didn't save. Your night isn't lost — try once more."
