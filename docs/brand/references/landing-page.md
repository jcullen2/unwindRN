# Landing Page & Web Copy

## Section hierarchy (fixed order — this order IS the positioning)

1. **Hero: the record.** Headline in the counting/knowing register. Subhead carries the one-sentence position. Visual: the logbook heatfield or a shift card — never the debrief/companion screen, never soft illustration. The hero must show accumulated work.
2. **The logbook / "the career nobody else counts."** Backfill story: tell it your history and the record starts where your career started (~1,153 shifts for a nurse eight years in). Career stats, milestone countdown, estimates wearing the ~. This is the emotional peak of the page.
3. **How the record gets written.** Taps + the elastic debrief. Frame the debrief in the aviation register ("what pilots do after a flight"). State elasticity: two minutes of taps has no ceiling — the voice companion is there when the night needs twenty minutes. Speed appears only as a "when" ("in the minutes after clock-out").
4. **The flywheel / what it gives back.** 3–4 example insight lines (career-facing only). Show the ramp honestly: record on day one, patterns by month three, self-knowledge by year one.
5. **Milestones & shift cards.** The shareable proof layer — Strava-card energy, celebratory. Show an actual card mock.
6. **The trust inversion.** Short, declarative: "No dashboard for your manager. No report to HR. No survey to score you. Your record — and it stops with you." Then the product-truth bullets (on-device voice, scrubber, export/delete).
7. **FAQ.** "Is this therapy?" lives here and only here. HIPAA/license question here. 988 in the answer and the footer.
8. **Closing CTA.** Record-framed: "Tonight deserves a record" is on-brand and may stay.

## Hero rules

- Kill test applies hardest here. No line Headspace could say.
- At least one number in the hero section.
- Candidate register: "Every shift counts. Now they're counted." / "The record your career deserves." / "1,153 shifts. Finally on the record." / "Your career, on the record — starting tonight."
- CTA: waitlist/beta framing stays plain ("Join the beta waitlist"), no urgency theatrics.

## Imagery

- Lead with: heatfield, logbook, shift cards, milestone screens, stat blocks.
- Demote or cut: lantern, moon, meditative illustration, "How was today?" screens in hero slots. The debrief screen may appear in section 3 only.
- Screenshots ordered by the same hierarchy: logbook → home stats → taps → debrief.

## Waitlist / email copy

- One-email promise stays verbatim in spirit: "One email when your invite is ready — that's the only one we'll send."
- Confirmation and invite emails follow the same voice: count something, promise nothing therapeutic, sign off plain.
