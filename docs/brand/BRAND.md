# unwindRN Brand System

This skill governs every word unwindRN says in public and in-app. It encodes hard-won positioning decisions. Do not relitigate them; execute them.

## The one-sentence position (memorize this)

> **unwindRN is how nurses know their own careers — every shift counted, every pattern surfaced, written in the minutes after clock-out.**

The record is the substrate. The knowledge is the product. The debrief is how it gets written.

## What unwindRN is and is not

unwindRN is **an app for nurses** — not a wellness app, not a mental health app, not a scheduling app. It is the nurse's app. Mental wellness is ONE feature among many (calendar, career progression, milestones, sharing, education, career pride, memories). It is real, it is never the headline.

The name reads as: **unwind** = the post-shift ritual (what professionals do after the mission — never a therapy verb). **RN** = registered nurse, and also "right now." Copy must reinforce the ritual/professional reading. If surrounding copy is soft, the name reads as Calm; if surrounding copy counts and shows the logbook, the name reads as ritual. The copy does the disambiguation.

## The strategy stack (ordering is the strategy)

1. **Position** — how nurses know their own careers.
2. **Mechanism (the flywheel)** — minutes in, career out, compounding. Log a shift → get back a record. Keep logging → get back patterns. Keep going → get back your career, legible and provable.
3. **Trust stance (the inversion)** — nurses are the most surveyed, least served workforce. Every survey sends data UP. unwindRN is the first record where the data flows TO the nurse and stops. Quiet declaratives, never combative: "No dashboard for your manager. No report to HR." This converts privacy from defense into offense.
4. **Engine (the elastic debrief)** — as short as your good nights, as long as your bad ones. Two minutes of taps, or twenty minutes with the voice companion when the night was a code and a loss. NEVER market session speed as a benefit. Duration is a property, not a pitch.
5. **Platform (everything else)** — calendar, education, memories, sharing: discovered inside the app, listed low or not at all in acquisition marketing. Market the wedge, deliver the platform.

## The moat (get this right)

The moat is **knowledge**: what the app learns to show the nurse about her own career — patterns, progress, milestones, trajectory. NOT ownership (ownership is the trust layer, a precondition), NOT the data itself (a pile of exportable data is a filing cabinet; knowledge compounds). After 200 debriefs the app can show a nurse things about her career she doesn't consciously know. That is unquittable.

**Flywheel language rules:** frame as *give and get back*, never *track and analyze*. The nurse is the knower; the app is the notebook that got smart. Say "you'll know your career," never "we know you." The app only knows what the nurse chose to tell it — nothing scraped, nothing inferred from her phone, nothing pulled from the hospital.

**The honest ramp** (never promise day-one intelligence over an empty logbook): instant record on day one via career backfill (~1,153 career shifts, estimates wear the ~), patterns by month three, self-knowledge by year one.

**Insight direction:** marketing leads with CAREER-facing insights (shift mix, charge shifts doubling, milestone countdowns, trajectory). SELF-facing insights (mood, fatigue patterns) exist in-app as discoveries, never as homepage promises — they drift the brand back toward wellness gravity.

## Voice: three enforceable rules

1. **Count, don't console.** Numbers, milestones, accumulation. "You held 14 nights this month" beats any sentence about how hard nights are. The empathy is implicit in the counting — the app noticing IS the care.
2. **Talk to a professional, not a patient.** Aviation/military register: debrief, logbook, record, held, on the record. Pilots debrief; surgeons debrief. Nurses are the toughest people in the building and every sentence assumes it. Never tender-to-the-point-of-fragile.
3. **State the trust inversion plainly.** "Yours. No hospital sees it." as load-bearing declaratives, not footnote reassurance.

**The kill test:** *Could Headspace say this line?* If yes, kill it. Headspace could say "put the shift down." It could never say "1,153 shifts."

## Vocabulary

**Use:** held, counted, record, logbook, debrief, on the record, shifts, hours held, heatfield, milestone, career, clock out, the floor, nights, your record, provable, earned.

**Ban:** self-care, wellness journey, healing, safe space, mindfulness, "put the shift down" as a primary frame (retired headline), any session-speed claim as a benefit ("twenty seconds" as an H2), "mental health" in headlines or App Store title/subtitle, therapy denials above the fold, streak/guilt mechanics anywhere ("you missed a day"), "we know you," "track/tracking" applied to the nurse herself, lantern-style meditation imagery in marketing hero slots.

**Allowed but demoted:** the "Is this therapy? No." answer lives in FAQ only — a real question deserves a real answer, but the brand never opens with a denial. 988 stays one tap away in-app and appears in footer/FAQ, never as a marketing feature.

## Non-negotiable product-truth claims (keep these accurate in all copy)

- Voice transcribed on-device; audio never uploaded or stored; only the approved entry is saved.
- Patient identifiers scrubbed by design; the app deflects patient info.
- Estimates wear the ~; the record is honest about counted vs. estimated.
- No streaks, no red badges; skipping is never punished.
- Export or delete everything, any time.
- Free during beta; no surprise paywalls on your own record.

## Worked examples (calibrate on these)

| Situation | Wrong (wellness register) | Right (unwindRN register) |
|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | "Put the shift down. Breathe." | "Every shift counts. Now they're counted." |
| Debrief feature | "A gentle space to process your feelings" | "The debrief: what pilots do after a flight. As short as your good nights, as long as your bad ones." |
| Insight line | "We've noticed you seem stressed lately" | "Your charge shifts doubled since March. That's a pattern worth a line on a resume." |
| Milestone | "Great job on your self-care streak!" | "Shift #1,500. Eleven years on the floor, on the record." |
| Privacy | "Your data is safe with us" | "No dashboard for your manager. No report to HR. Your record, and it stops with you." |
| Notification | "Don't forget to check in tonight! 🧘" | "Night 3 of 3 in the books? Two minutes and it's kept." |
| Speed | "Log your shift in just 20 seconds!" | "Written in the minutes after clock-out." |

## Review checklist (run on any copy before shipping)

1. Does the piece lead with record/knowledge/career, with wellness as at most a supporting feature?
2. Kill test passed — could Headspace say any line? (Rewrite those.)
3. Any session-speed claims sold as benefits? (Convert to a "when," not a "why.")
4. Any therapy denials or defensive trust content above the fold? (Demote to FAQ; restate trust as the inversion.)
5. Does flywheel language say "you'll know" rather than "we know"? Is the day-one ramp honest (backfill → patterns → self-knowledge)?
6. Professional register throughout — would a charge nurse with twelve years on nights feel spoken to as a peer?
7. Numbers present? Copy without at least one counted thing is usually off-brand.
8. Product-truth claims accurate against the list above?
